2. Information We Collect

We collect and process the following types of information:

2.1 Login Information

When you sign up for our app, we process the following data, depending on your chosen login method:

  * Google Login: We receive your email address and your name (or display name) from Google. This information is used to create and manage your user account. Your Google login credentials themselves are not stored by us.

  * Anonymous Login: We generate an anonymous user ID that allows you to use the app without providing personal data. This ID is not directly linkable to your person.

  * Email/Password Login: We store your email address and a hashed version of your password (which is not readable in plain text).

2.2 Tapas Data

This refers to the data you actively enter or generate within the app related to "Tapas." Examples might include:

  * Tapas names, creation date, start date/time, tapas duration, description, goals, individual parts, etc.

  * Tapas history: confirmed days, days made up/made up, status (successful/failed), reason for failure, results

  * This data is associated with your user account to provide the core functionality of the app (e.g., tracking your tapas experiences, sharing with friends if such a feature exists).

2.3 Cookies

Our application uses cookies managed by the Next.js plugin cookies-next. These cookies serve exclusively functional purposes to ensure the proper operation of the app and to enhance your user experience. Examples of how these cookies are used include:

  * Storing your login session so you don't have to re-enter login information on every visit.

  * Managing states within the application that are necessary for navigation and interaction.

We do not use special cookies, tracking cookies for marketing purposes, or cookies for web analytics or advertising purposes.

2.4 No Analytics, No Ads

  * We do not use any analytics services (such as Google Analytics or similar) to track your behavior within the app.

  * No advertisements are displayed in our app, and we do not share your data with third parties for advertising purposes.

  * Legitimate Interests (Art. 6 (1) lit. f GDPR): The processing of functional cookies and certain technical data (e.g., for security or troubleshooting) serves our legitimate interest in maintaining and improving the security and functionality of our app.

8. Your Data Protection Rights (GDPR)

Under the GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  * Right of Access (Art. 15 GDPR): You have the right to obtain confirmation as to whether or not personal data concerning you are being processed, and, where that is the case, access to the personal data.

  * Right to Rectification (Art. 16 GDPR): You have the right to obtain the rectification of inaccurate personal data concerning you without undue delay.

  * Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten") (Art. 17 GDPR): You have the right to request the erasure of your personal data under certain conditions.

  * Right to Restriction of Processing (Art. 18 GDPR): You have the right to request the restriction of the processing of your data under certain conditions.

  * Right to Data Portability (Art. 20 GDPR): You have the right to receive the personal data concerning you, which you have provided to us, in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format and have the right to transmit those data to another controller.

  * Right to Object (Art. 21 GDPR): You have the right to object, on grounds relating to your particular situation, at any time to processing of personal data concerning you.

  * Right to Withdraw Consent (Art. 7 (3) GDPR): Where processing is based on your consent, you have the right to withdraw your consent at any time. The withdrawal of consent shall not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.

  * Right to Lodge a Complaint with a Supervisory Authority (Art. 77 GDPR): Without prejudice to any other administrative or judicial remedy, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority, in particular in the Member State of your habitual residence, place of work or place of the alleged infringement if you consider that the processing of personal data relating to you infringes the GDPR.
